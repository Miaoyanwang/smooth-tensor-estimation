\begin{figure}[H]
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=.7\textwidth]{figure/figure2.pdf}
\end{center}
\caption{\small Statistical and computational properties for (i) biclustering and (ii) high-order clustering with an order-$d$ $(p,\ldots,p)$-dimensional tensor. The signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) is divided into three regions according to different performance behaviors. Region (A): statistically and computationally easy, where the problem is solvable in polynomial time. Region (B): statistically hard, where no consistent estimator exists even with unbounded computational resources. Region (C): statistically easy but computationally hard, where we conjecture that no polynomial algorithm exists for the problem. Remarkably, a statistical-computational gap (i.e., region C) arises only for tensors of order 3 or greater. }
\end{figure}
